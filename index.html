An Automated Method to Correct Artifacts in Neural Text-to-speech Models



Abstract

Recent advances in speech synthesis models have led to remarkable progress in producing speech that resembles human speech. However, these models still face challenges including making speech without errors. Existing artifact correction methods are inefficient as they require additional training data and a separate model to generate corrected speech. This paper introduces a new approach to address these issues by detecting and correcting defective units that contribute to speech artifacts using a neural network interpretability method. By automatically correcting defective units to be within the normal distribution, we achieved significant improvements in attention errors and quality of synthesized speech without additional data or training. Our results demonstrate the effectiveness of the interpretability method in identifying and correcting defects in speech synthesis models, providing researchers with a new approach to enhance the stability and quality of speech synthesis systems.


Artificial speech correction with the proposed method

We offer four types of sample audio: normal, artifact, layer-wise relevance propagation (LRP), and random. The artifact samples is generated abnormally, with the incorrect portions highlighted in red. By using the LRP method to correct the defective units, various types of artifact speech can be corrected into normal speech naturally. However, there is no improvement when the units are corrected randomly.
